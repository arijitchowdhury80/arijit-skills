# Algolia Branded Partner Co-Marketing Materials

Create co-branded materials for technology partners and system integrators. Manages dual-brand compliance, joint messaging hierarchy, and co-marketing best practices while maintaining Algolia brand integrity.

## Input

- Partner company name and type (technology partner, SI partner, agency partner, marketplace partner)
- Material type (joint solution brief, integration overview, co-branded one-pager, partner landing page, co-sell battle card)
- Joint value proposition (what the combined solution delivers)
- Integration details (how the products work together)
- Shared customer examples or results (if available)
- Partner brand guidelines reference (colors, logo, voice -- if provided)
- Target audience (partner's customers, Algolia's customers, joint prospects)

## Process

1. **Co-Brand Framework** -- Establish logo hierarchy: for Algolia-led materials, Algolia logo appears first/left/top; for joint materials, both logos at equal size side by side. Define color usage: primary sections use Algolia brand colors, partner sections can use partner colors in designated areas. Neutral sections use Space Gray on white.
2. **Joint Value Proposition** -- Write a combined headline that names both companies and the joint outcome. Format: "[Partner] + Algolia: [Benefit Statement]". The value proposition must be greater than either product alone -- explain the integration multiplier.
3. **Joint Solution Brief** -- Two to three pages: (1) Cover with both logos and joint headline, (2) Challenge section framing the problem the joint solution solves, (3) Solution architecture showing how the products integrate, (4) Benefits section with three to four joint value points, (5) Customer proof point if available, (6) CTA for both companies.
4. **Integration Overview** -- One page: architecture diagram description, integration method (API, webhook, plugin, native), data flow summary, setup complexity estimate, supported platforms and versions. Technical but accessible to a solutions architect audience.
5. **Co-Branded One-Pager** -- Follow the `/algolia-one-pager` format with dual branding: both logos in header, joint key message, split content blocks (Algolia capabilities and partner capabilities), combined metrics, dual CTAs.
6. **Partner Landing Page** -- Follow the `/algolia-landing` format with partner co-branding: joint hero with both logos, combined value proposition, integration feature blocks, shared customer proof, dual CTA (try Algolia and try partner).
7. **Co-Sell Battle Card** -- Internal sales tool: joint ICP definition, discovery questions that surface joint need, competitive positioning (how the joint solution beats alternatives), objection handling for the partner angle, pricing guidance notes, deal registration process.
8. **Messaging Hierarchy** -- In all materials: lead with the joint customer outcome, then explain each company's contribution. Neither brand should dominate the narrative. Use "together" and "combined" language.
9. **Legal Review Notes** -- Flag any claims that need partner legal approval. Note co-marketing agreement requirements. Mark sections that require partner review before publication.
10. **Run `/algolia-brand-check`** on the Algolia-branded sections of all materials before finalizing.

## Output Sections

### Co-Brand Specifications
- Logo placement rules, color usage zones, font hierarchy

### Joint Value Proposition
- Combined headline, supporting statement, elevator pitch (30 seconds)

### Material Content
Depending on type selected:
- **Joint Solution Brief**: Cover, challenge, solution, benefits, proof, CTA
- **Integration Overview**: Architecture, data flow, setup, platforms
- **Co-Branded One-Pager**: Header, key message, content blocks, metrics, CTA
- **Partner Landing Page**: Hero, features, proof, dual CTA
- **Co-Sell Battle Card**: ICP, discovery questions, positioning, objections, process

### Partner Review Checklist
- Sections requiring partner approval
- Brand guideline compliance notes for both brands
- Legal review items

### Distribution Plan
- Where to host (Algolia site, partner site, shared portal)
- Sales enablement delivery method
- Update cadence

## Brand Requirements

- **Voice**: Collaborative and mutual -- partner materials celebrate the joint solution. Neither brand is subordinate. Use "together", "combined", "joint" language. Algolia voice remains confident and credible.
- **Colors**: Algolia-led sections use Nebula Blue #003DFF and Space Gray #21243D. Partner sections may use partner brand colors in designated zones. Shared sections use neutral palette (Space Gray on white). Algolia Purple #5468FF for accent elements in Algolia sections only.
- **Font**: Source Sans Pro for Algolia-branded sections. Partner font acceptable in partner-designated areas. Joint sections default to Source Sans Pro.
- **Logo Rules**: Both logos at equal visual weight in joint materials. Minimum clear space maintained for both. Never combine logos into a single mark. Use "+" or "and" between logos, not merged graphics.
- **Product Names**: Always use official Algolia product names. Reference partner products by their official names as well.
- **Stats**: Algolia-specific stats require Algolia approval. Partner-specific stats require partner approval. Joint metrics require both.
- **Competitors**: No competitor names in any partner-facing or customer-facing content. Internal battle cards may reference competitors for positioning context.
- **Legal**: All co-branded materials require review from both companies before external distribution
