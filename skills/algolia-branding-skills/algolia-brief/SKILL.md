# Algolia Branded Campaign Brief

Create a comprehensive campaign planning document that aligns Marketing, ABX, and Sales teams on target audience, messaging, channels, content, timeline, and success metrics. Follows Algolia brand standards throughout.

## Input

- Campaign name and type (product launch, ABX play, demand gen, content series, event, brand awareness)
- Business objective (pipeline generation, brand awareness, product adoption, customer expansion)
- Target audience or account list criteria
- Budget range (if known)
- Timeline constraints (launch date, event date, quarter end)
- Algolia products or features to promote
- Existing assets or content to leverage

## Process

1. **Campaign Overview** -- Write a one-paragraph executive summary: what, why, who, when, expected outcome. This should be readable in 30 seconds by a VP.
2. **Business Objective** -- Define the primary KPI and one to two supporting metrics. Tie to quarterly or annual goals. Be specific: "Generate 50 MQLs from enterprise accounts in financial services" not "increase awareness."
3. **Target Audience** -- Define the ICP for this campaign: industry, company size, job titles, technographic signals, behavioral triggers. For ABX: specify account list criteria and expected list size. Map primary and secondary personas.
4. **Key Messages** -- Create a three-tier messaging framework: (1) Campaign headline -- one sentence, benefit-driven, (2) Supporting messages -- three to four value propositions with proof points, (3) Persona-specific angles -- tailored messaging for each target persona. All messaging must align with Algolia brand voice.
5. **Channel Strategy** -- Map messages to channels: paid (LinkedIn Ads, Google Ads, display), owned (blog, email, social, website), earned (PR, analyst relations, community). Specify budget allocation percentages across channels.
6. **Content Requirements** -- List every content asset needed: blog posts, emails, landing pages, social posts, ads, one-pagers, decks, videos. For each: format, owner, due date, status. Reference other Algolia brand skills for creation (e.g., `/algolia-blog`, `/algolia-email`, `/algolia-landing`).
7. **ABX Integration** -- If ABX play: define account tiers (1:1, 1:few, 1:many), personalization depth per tier, sales-marketing handoff triggers, SDR talk track bullet points.
8. **Timeline** -- Week-by-week project plan from brief approval to campaign wrap. Include content creation deadlines, review cycles, launch dates, and reporting milestones.
9. **Budget Breakdown** -- Allocate budget across: paid media, content production, tools/platforms, events, contingency. Include cost-per-lead targets.
10. **Success Metrics** -- Define primary KPI, secondary metrics, and reporting cadence. Include: target MQLs, pipeline generated, conversion rates, engagement metrics, cost-per-lead, ROI calculation framework.
11. **Approval Workflow** -- List stakeholders who must approve: messaging (Brand), audience (Demand Gen), budget (Marketing Ops), timeline (Program Manager). Include review deadlines.
12. **Run `/algolia-brand-check`** on all campaign messaging before finalizing the brief.

## Output Sections

### Executive Summary
- One-paragraph campaign overview

### Objective & KPIs
- Primary KPI with target, supporting metrics

### Target Audience
- ICP criteria, persona profiles, account list parameters

### Messaging Framework
- Campaign headline, supporting messages, persona-specific angles

### Channel Strategy
- Channel list with budget allocation and message mapping

### Content Plan
- Asset list with format, owner, due date, brand skill reference

### ABX Integration (if applicable)
- Tier definitions, personalization plan, handoff triggers

### Timeline
- Week-by-week project plan

### Budget
- Line-item budget with cost-per-lead targets

### Success Metrics
- KPI targets, reporting cadence, ROI framework

### Approvals
- Stakeholder list with review deadlines

## Brand Requirements

- **Voice**: Strategic and precise -- briefs are internal documents but still reflect Algolia brand standards. Messaging sections must be publication-ready.
- **Colors**: Use Algolia brand colors in any visual mockups or examples: Nebula Blue #003DFF, Space Gray #21243D, Algolia Purple #5468FF
- **Font**: Source Sans Pro for any formatted output or mockups
- **Messaging**: All customer-facing messages must pass brand check: no competitor names, no unsubstantiated claims, no anthropomorphism, approved stats only
- **Product Names**: Use official product names in all messaging: Algolia Search, Algolia Recommend, Algolia AI Search
- **Stats**: Campaign materials may only claim approved metrics (17,000+ customers, 1.7T searches/year, 30B records) unless using attributed customer results
- **Competitors**: Internal sections may reference competitors for positioning context; all customer-facing content must use category terms
- **Approval**: Note that all customer-facing messaging requires brand team sign-off before production
