# Algolia Editorial Standards & Guidelines

## Foundation

Algolia follows **AP Style** with **Merriam-Webster** as the dictionary reference. All public-facing marketing content, website copy, and promotional collaterals use **American English**.

## Numbers

### General Rules
- **Spell out** numbers one through nine
- **Use numerals** for 10 and above
- **Exception**: OK to use numerals for 1–9 if content is mathematically oriented or technical
- **Spell out** numbers when they begin a sentence: "Ninety-nine percent of users say..."
- If an expression typically uses spelled-out numbers, leave them: "a third-party integration"
- Numbers with 3+ digits include commas: 1,000
- Space-constrained abbreviation: use "k" for thousands (1k, 150k)

### Dates
- Spell out days of the week and month names: "Saturday, January 24"
- Abbreviate only if space is an issue

### Fractions & Decimals
- Spell out fractions: "two-thirds"
- Exception: numerals OK in technical content: ⅔
- Use decimal points when fractions are unwieldy: 1.375

### Percentages
- Use the percent symbol: 40%

### Number Ranges
- Use an en dash (–) without spaces: 5–10

### Time of Day
- Use am/pm with a space: 5 pm
- Time ranges with en dash, include am/pm for each: 3:00 pm–4:00 pm
- Date and time together — use "at" not a comma: "Thursday, October 15 at 2:00 pm"

## Punctuation

### Commas
- **Always use the Oxford/serial comma**: "Algolia specializes in search, discovery, and recommendations"

### Colons
- Introduce a bulleted list with a phrase ending in a colon: "This section covers:"
- Don't capitalize the first word after a colon unless it's a proper noun

### Forward Slashes
- Avoid words separated by forward slashes (e.g., "and/or") unless space is constrained

### Hyphens and Dashes
| Mark | Name | Usage | Example |
|------|------|-------|---------|
| - | Hyphen | Join compound adjectives before a noun | "first-time user" |
| – | En dash | Indicate ranges | "Monday–Friday" |
| — | Em dash | Parenthetical statements (with spaces on each side) | "Multivariate testing — just one of our features — can help you" |

### Lists (Bulleted or Numbered)
- **No punctuation** at the end of single-phrase list items (treat as fragments)
- **If one list item** contains two or more sentences, include periods in ALL list items
- Use **bulleted lists** when item sequence doesn't matter
- Use **numbered lists** when sequence matters (step-by-step instructions)

### Periods
- Leave off the period in titles, headings, and subheadings
- Exception: include a question mark if the phrase is a question

## Emphasis Formatting

### Bold
- Sparingly bold key and summarizing phrases
- Don't bold SEO keywords
- Bold punctuation adjacent to a bolded word or phrase

### Italic
- Use only to emphasize an important word or phrase
- Don't italicize to simply call attention (use bold instead)

### Colored Text
- Use only in special circumstances (e.g., graphs)

### Underlining
- Use only in links (automatically added)

## Job Titles
- **Capitalize before name**: "Content Strategist Jane Smith"
- **Lowercase after name**: "Jane Smith, content strategist"
- **Lowercase as common nouns**: "The content strategist designed the architecture"

## Web Elements

### Buttons
- Capitalize names of buttons

### Forms
- Use sentence case for form titles and field names

### Links
- Select minimal text (only the noun) — don't include articles (a, an, the, our)
- Don't include the comma or period if a link is at the end of a sentence
- Don't link to competitors' websites

### Menus
- Title case for menu names
- Sentence case for menu items

### Titles, Headings, Subheadings
- Don't use punctuation to end (exception: question marks)
- Use hierarchical headings: H1, H2, H3

## Anthropomorphism Rules
- **Never** ascribe human abilities to technologies or entities
- **Don't say**: "Brands are looking at ways..." or "Semantic search looks to change this..."
- **Do say**: "Business leaders are exploring..." or "Semantic search is changing..."
