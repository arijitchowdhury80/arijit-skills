# Algolia Social Media Policy & Guidelines

## Philosophy

Algolia believes in open communication and encourages employees to share their passion. Social media gives every employee a platform to tell the world about Algolia.

## Three Core Expectations for Every Employee Online

1. **Disclose your relationship to Algolia**
2. **Protect Algolia**
3. **Use common sense**

## Official Social Media Account Purposes

Algolia maintains official social media accounts to:
- Build brand and company awareness
- Promote products and services
- Support internal communication efforts
- Reinforce recruitment efforts
- Provide an inside view into life at Algolia
- Build community and encourage employee participation and advocacy
- Reinforce messaging about the value of products, employees, and business partners

## Guidelines for All Employees

### Language & Channels
- Post/comment in the language of the channel
- Global channels use English; country-based channels may use local languages
- Don't mix languages in a single post

### Representing Algolia
- When online, you represent Algolia — our people, our values
- No room for bigotry, prejudice, misogyny, or hatred
- Be aware of religious/cultural holidays, world events, and tragedies for appropriateness and tone
- Don't post anything that might make followers feel isolated or not included

### Tagging & Hashtags
- When writing about Algolia or its products, include **#Algolia** or tag **@Algolia**
- This lets people know you are affiliated with Algolia

### Personal Responsibility
- You are personally responsible for content you publish (even ghostwritten content)
- The internet never forgets — everything is visible for a very long time
- If something makes you even slightly uncomfortable — don't publish it
- When unsure about Algolia-related content, contact the Social Media team

### Confidentiality
- **Never post confidential information** on any social platform
- If Algolia hasn't officially released information, don't discuss it
- This includes:
  - Financial information
  - Product information and roadmaps
  - Customer information
  - Partner information
  - Legal information
- **Never discuss financial topics or predictions of future performance**

### Professional Conduct
- Don't criticize Algolia or competitors — take the high road
- Everything published must be true and not misleading
- All claims must be substantiated and approved
- Avoid content from obscure or unsubstantiated sources
- Statistics must come from official internal sources (not sites like Owler)

### Corrections
- If you posted something inaccurate, be the first to respond and correct it
- If modifying a blog post, make it clear you've done so

### Copyright & Images
- All images must be original (Algolia Creative/Social team) or licensed (Adobe Stock, Shutterstock)
- Never take images from Google Image search and add the Algolia logo
- Attribute non-original content and link to originators
- Attribution does not necessarily prevent copyright violation

### Brand Consistency
- All policies regarding Algolia logos, names, and branding apply to social media
- Use official brand assets only

### Media Inquiries
- If journalists or industry influencers contact you based on social activity, notify Algolia's PR or Social Media team immediately

## Channel-Specific Guidelines

### LinkedIn
- Professional, thought leadership tone
- Banner size: 1128 x 284 px
- Tag: @Algolia
- Focus: Industry insights, company news, career content, customer stories

### X (Twitter)
- Concise, engaging, conversational
- Banner size: 1500 x 500 px
- Tag: @Algolia
- Hashtag: #Algolia
- Focus: Product updates, event coverage, developer tips, industry commentary

### Facebook
- Community-oriented, accessible
- Banner size: 851 x 315 px
- Focus: Company culture, events, broader audience content

### Instagram
- Visual-first, behind-the-scenes
- Focus: Company culture, events, team highlights

### Reddit
- Authentic, technical, helpful
- Never be promotional — provide genuine value
- Focus: Developer community engagement, technical Q&A
